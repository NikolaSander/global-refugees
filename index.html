About the Data

What is the difference between migrants, refugees and asylum applicants? Migrants are people who live outside their country of birth. Most migrants move voluntarily for reasons related to education, employment and family. However, some migrants move solely to avoid prosecution or punishment on religious or political grounds. These migrants are given refugee status by another country if their asylum application is successfull. Hence, every refugee is a migrant, but not every migrant is a refugee.// This visualisation shows the flows of migrants, refugees and asylum applicants. A total of 244 million international MIGRANTS lived outside their country of birth in 2015. The numbers have been estimated by the United Nations. A small part of the global stock of international migrants are the 21.3 million REFUGEES who have been granted asylum by the host country under the Geneva Convention at the end of 2015. The refugees have already completed the process of applying for asylum, which often takes more than a year. A large share of those people who arrived in Europe in 2015 did not have refugee status at the end of 2015 because of the long processing time. The UNHCR estimates that 2.0 million first time ASYLUM APPLICATIONS were lodged in 2015.

About the plot

The circular plot shows the origins and destinations of international migrants, refugees and asylum applicants around the world. Country-to-country flows of less than 3000 persons are not shown so as to avoid visual clutter. The window that pops up when hovering over the plot shows the number of people in 2015.

How to read the plot

How to read the plot

Origins and destinations are represented by the circle’s segments. Each region/country is assigned a colour. Flows have the same colour as their origin and the width indicates their size. The direction of the flow is also shown by the gap between flow and country/region: small gap indicates origin; large gap indicates destination. Click on a segment to switch between country and region view.

Switch between time periods using Ctrl and number keys (1,2,3,4); reset the plot using Ctrl and r; zoom in/out with Ctrl and +/-
